Education: United States Naval Academy

I studied Applied Mathematics and Mechanical Engineering as a Midshipman at the Naval Academy from 2016 to 2020.

During this time, I had the opportunity to serve in a variety of Leadership Roles and conduct a Year-long Research Project with three faculty advisers.

I achieved a GPA of 4.0 while pursuing a double-major, and finished my career as a Midshipman ranked First in my class in academics.

I was selected as one of nine Trident Scholars for my class, and developed machine-learning models for Near-Maritime Optical Turbulence.

Proficiencies

Skills and Experience

Data Analytics:
I had the opportunity to capture, develop, and investigate synchronized time-series data sets for environmental modeling, rapid structural prototyping, and image-based corrosion classification.
I learned the importance of domain knowledge in data pre-processing, and a variety of approaches to handling missing data in large quantitative data sets.

Mechanical Engineering Design:
For my team Capstone design project, I applied rapid prototyping and computer aided design to develop a semi-autonomous fluid pipe inspection robot.
The project was entered into the National Association of Corrosion Engineers 2019 Student Design Competition. The USNA team which I lead placed first in the competition, successfully identifying and classifying all instances of corrosion within a steel fluid pipe.

Machine Learning Model Development:
I have experience in both regression and classification problems, trained on data sets which I developed individually or as part of a larger team.
For my Capstone design project, I lead the effort to train and tune an image classifier for corrosion classification used on a semi-autonomous inspection robot.
During my year-long Trident research project, I investigated non-parametric ensemble decision tree models and convolutional neural network models for quantitative optical turbulence prediction.
These models and associated findings were submitted to the Defense Technical Information Center. and Applied Optics journal, and are under peer-review for future publication as of May 2020.
